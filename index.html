Our approach

Four restoration pillars, one living system.

Aaniver treats every project as part of a connected ecological network. Water security, forest health, river recovery, and wildlife movement all strengthen one another.

Community first

Local residents, volunteers, and partner institutions co-design the work so each intervention stays rooted in real need and long-term care.

Science-backed

We prioritize native planting, recharge mapping, pollution source control, and measurable outcome tracking instead of one-time cleanups.

01

Water recharge

Reviving ponds, lakes, and rain-harvesting systems where water stress is rising fastest.

02

Native forestry

Rebuilding biodiversity with indigenous species, Miyawaki patches, and green corridors that fit local soil and rainfall patterns.

03

River health

Reducing waste inflow, improving water quality, and supporting long-horizon restoration for riverbanks and catchment zones.

04

Wildlife safety

Protecting habitat edges, building rescue networks, and reducing conflict where people and wildlife share shrinking boundaries.

Founder's philosophy

மண்ணோடு இணைந்த சிந்தனை

"பூமி நமதல்ல; நாம் பூமியின் பிள்ளைகள்." "The earth does not belong to us. We belong to the earth. Every river we heal heals us too."

SanthaKumar founded Aaniver with a simple belief: environmental repair has to feel local, practical, and collective if it is going to last. Every restoration effort is treated not as an isolated campaign, but as a long relationship between land, water, and people.

SanthaKumar Founder, Aaniver

Journey

வேர் முதல் இயக்கம் வரை

How Aaniver has grown from intent to movement.

2019

Foundation Laid

SanthaKumar established Aaniver with a focused mission to restore water bodies and forests across Tamil Nadu's stressed districts.

2020

First Lake Revival

Completed desilting and cleanup of 3 water bodies in Coimbatore district, restoring groundwater recharge for over 800 families.

2022

Forest Drive Begins

Launched the Miyawaki urban forestry programme, planting 5,000+ native saplings across Tiruppur and Erode with community volunteers.

2024

Regional Expansion

Scaled to 6 districts, 38+ active projects, and 12,000+ volunteers, with recognition for pond restoration work in Somaiyampalayam.
